UTM Naming Convention Guide

Standardized Framework for Campaign Tracking Across Teams

Why UTM conventions matter: Consistent UTM naming is the foundation of accurate campaign analytics. Without standards, your Google Analytics data becomes unusable within weeks. This guide provides a battle-tested framework used by marketing teams managing 100+ campaigns simultaneously.

Core Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Rule #1: Always Use Lowercase
Google Analytics treats "Facebook" and "facebook" as different sources. Use lowercase exclusively to avoid data fragmentation.

‚úÖ utm_source=facebook
‚ùå utm_source=Facebook
Rule #2: Use Hyphens, Not Spaces or Underscores
Spaces become %20 in URLs (ugly and hard to read). Underscores can be confused with spaces. Hyphens are clean and URL-safe.

‚úÖ utm_campaign=spring-sale-2024
‚ùå utm_campaign=spring_sale_2024
‚ùå utm_campaign=Spring%20Sale%202024
Rule #3: Never Change Source Mid-Campaign
If you start with utm_source=facebook, don't switch to utm_source=fb or utm_source=meta halfway through. Pick one and stick with it forever.
Rule #4: Document Everything
Keep a shared spreadsheet of all active campaigns with their UTM parameters. This prevents duplicates and ensures team alignment.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Mistake #1: Inconsistent Capitalization
Using "Facebook," "facebook," and "fb" creates three separate sources in analytics. Pick one (lowercase) and never deviate.
‚ùå Mistake #2: Using UTMs on Internal Links
Never add UTM parameters to links within your own site. This resets the session and attributes conversions incorrectly. UTMs are for external traffic only.
‚ùå Mistake #3: Overly Generic Campaign Names
"campaign1" or "test" tells you nothing in 3 months. Use descriptive names: "spring-sale-q2" or "webinar-jan-2024."
‚ùå Mistake #4: Not Testing Links Before Launch
Always click your UTM links and verify they appear correctly in Google Analytics Real-Time reports before spending ad budget.

Maintain a master spreadsheet with columns: Campaign Name, Start Date, End Date, Full UTM Link, Budget, Owner. This prevents duplicate campaign names and provides historical reference.
üí° Tip #4: Audit Quarterly
Every quarter, review your GA4 source/medium report. Look for inconsistencies (e.g., "facebook" vs "fb"). Clean up naming going forward.
üí° Tip #5: Shorten Links for Social
UTM links are long and ugly. Use a link shortener (Bitly, Rebrandly) for social posts, but keep the full UTM link in your tracking spreadsheet.
