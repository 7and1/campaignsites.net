Landing Page Launch Checklist

50-Point Pre-Launch Audit for High-Converting Campaign Pages

How to use this checklist: Review each item before launching your campaign landing page. A "yes" to all critical items (marked ‚ö†Ô∏è) means you're ready to drive traffic. Optional items boost performance but aren't blockers.

1. Above the Fold (Critical ‚ö†Ô∏è)

Single, clear value proposition in headline
Headline communicates the core benefit in 12 words or less. Visitor should understand "what's in it for me" in 3 seconds.
Message match with ad copy
Headline or subheadline mirrors the exact promise from your ad. If ad says "Free Trial," page says "Start Your Free Trial."
One primary CTA above the fold
Single, high-contrast button with action-oriented copy. Remove competing CTAs or secondary actions from hero section.
Hero image or video supports the offer
Visual shows the product in use, the outcome, or the target customer. Avoid generic stock photos.
Trust signal visible immediately
Logo bar, testimonial snippet, or security badge appears within first screen. Builds credibility instantly.

2. Social Proof & Trust

Customer logos or brand names
Show 5-8 recognizable brands or customer logos. Include company size if relevant (e.g., "Trusted by 500+ SaaS companies").
Specific testimonials with attribution
Include full name, photo, company, and role. Avoid anonymous quotes. Highlight quantified results when possible.
Quantified social proof
"Join 10,000+ marketers" or "4.8/5 stars from 2,000 reviews." Numbers build credibility.
Security badges near form
SSL, payment processor logos, or privacy certifications. Place directly below or beside form fields.
Case study or success story
One detailed example with before/after metrics. "How [Company] increased conversions by 47% in 30 days."

3. Copy & Messaging

Benefit-focused, not feature-focused
Lead with outcomes. "Save 10 hours per week" beats "Automated scheduling tool."
Scannable bullet points (3-5 key benefits)
Short, parallel structure. Each bullet answers "What do I get?" or "What problem does this solve?"
Urgency or scarcity element
Deadline, limited seats, or expiring bonus. Must be genuine. "Offer ends Friday" or "Only 12 spots left."
Objection handling
Address top 2-3 concerns. "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime," "30-day money-back guarantee."
Clear, action-oriented CTA copy
"Get My Free Audit" beats "Submit." Use first-person language and be specific about what happens next.

4. Form Optimization (Critical ‚ö†Ô∏è)

Minimum required fields only
For lead gen: name + email. For demos: add company + role. Every extra field reduces conversions by ~5-10%.
Clear field labels and placeholders
Label above field, placeholder shows example. "Email Address" label with "you@company.com" placeholder.
Privacy statement near submit button
"We respect your privacy. Unsubscribe anytime." Reduces form anxiety.
Error validation and helpful messages
Real-time validation. "Please enter a valid email" instead of generic "Error."
Mobile-optimized form fields
Large tap targets (44px minimum), appropriate keyboard types (email, tel, number).

5. Mobile Experience (Critical ‚ö†Ô∏è)

Headline, CTA, and proof visible without scrolling
Test on actual devices. Core message must be clear on first screen.
Tap targets at least 44x44 pixels
Buttons, links, and form fields must be easy to tap. Add spacing between clickable elements.
Readable font sizes (16px minimum for body)
Avoid forcing users to pinch-zoom. Headlines 24px+, body 16px+.
Fast load time on 3G (under 3 seconds)
Test with Chrome DevTools throttling. Compress images, minimize scripts.
No horizontal scrolling
All content fits viewport width. Test on iPhone SE (smallest common screen).

6. Technical & Performance

Page load under 2 seconds
Use PageSpeed Insights or GTmetrix. Every second of delay costs ~7% conversions.
Images optimized and compressed
WebP format preferred. Hero image under 200KB. Use lazy loading for below-fold images.
SSL certificate installed (HTTPS)
Required for trust and SEO. Browsers flag HTTP pages as "Not Secure."
Form submission works correctly
Test end-to-end: submit form, verify email delivery, check CRM integration.
Thank you page or confirmation message
Clear next steps. "Check your inbox" or "We'll contact you within 24 hours."

7. Tracking & Analytics (Critical ‚ö†Ô∏è)

Google Analytics or tracking pixel installed
Verify with Tag Assistant or browser console. Track pageviews and events.
Conversion goal configured
Set up form submission as goal in GA4 or ad platform. Track conversion rate.
UTM parameters on all traffic sources
Every ad, email, and social link needs utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign. Use consistent naming.
Event tracking on CTA clicks
Track button clicks even if form isn't submitted. Helps identify drop-off points.
Scroll depth tracking
See how far visitors scroll. Identifies if key content is being seen.

8. Final Pre-Launch Checks

Cross-browser testing (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)
Test on actual browsers, not just emulators. Check form submission in each.
Proofread all copy for typos
Read backwards, use Grammarly, have someone else review. Typos kill credibility.
All links work (no 404s)
Click every link, button, and navigation item. Test privacy policy, terms, and footer links.
Meta tags for social sharing
Set og:title, og:description, og:image. Preview with Facebook Debugger and Twitter Card Validator.
5-second test with fresh eyes
Show page to someone unfamiliar. Can they explain the offer in 5 seconds? If not, simplify.
üí° Pro Tip: Post-Launch Optimization
After launch, monitor these metrics weekly: conversion rate, bounce rate, average time on page, and scroll depth. Run A/B tests on headline, CTA copy, and form length. Small improvements compound: a 10% lift in conversion rate can double your ROI over time.
